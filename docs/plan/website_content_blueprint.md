### 4.7 Specialist Clinics (American Hospital Dubai)

**Eyebrow:** `Specialist Clinics · American Hospital Dubai`  
**Headline:** `Five clinics. One coordinated team.`  
**Lead paragraph:** Complex cardiac conditions rarely sit inside one specialty. Dr. Narayanan leads multidisciplinary clinics that bring pulmonologists, neurologists, hepatologists and electrophysiologists into the same room — so families don't have to coordinate them.

#### Clinic 01 — Pulmonary Hypertension
- **Subtitle:** Elevated pulmonary arterial pressure
- **Body:** Long-term medical management for adults and children with pulmonary hypertension, including patients with shunt-related, idiopathic, and connective-tissue-driven disease.
- **Collaborates with:** Pulmonologists, Rheumatologists

#### Clinic 02 — Neuromuscular Disorders
- **Subtitle:** Cardiac care alongside the neurology team
- **Body:** Cardiomyopathy and conduction surveillance for children and adults with Duchenne, Becker, myotonic dystrophy and related neuromuscular conditions.
- **Collaborates with:** Neurologists

#### Clinic 03 — Fontan Clinic
- **Subtitle:** Lifelong follow-up after single-ventricle palliation
- **Body:** Tailored surveillance, exercise prescription and intervention planning for adolescents and adults living with Fontan circulation.
- **Collaborates with:** Hepatology, Adult Cardiology

#### Clinic 04 — Sports Cardiology
- **Subtitle:** Pre-participation screening for athletes
- **Body:** ECG, echo and exercise-test pathways for competitive and recreational athletes — including assessment of murmurs, syncope and exertional symptoms. Dubai has a growing community of athletes and active individuals. The goal is to help patients stay active safely, not restrict activity unnecessarily.
- **Collaborates with:** Sports Medicine, GPs

#### Clinic 05 — Adult Congenital Heart Disease (ACHD)
- **Subtitle:** Lifelong care for adults living with congenital conditions
- **Body:** Continuity from paediatric services into adulthood — pregnancy planning, arrhythmia surveillance, re-intervention and structural follow-up. Many children with congenital heart disease now live well into adulthood but require ongoing specialist care.
- **Collaborates with:** Obstetrics, Electrophysiology

### 4.13 When Should You See a Cardiologist?

**Eyebrow:** `Patient Guidance`  
**Headline:** `When to seek a cardiac evaluation`

**Body:** Early assessment provides reassurance, or timely treatment if needed. You should consider a cardiac evaluation if you or your child has:

- Known congenital heart disease or heart murmur
- Chest pain, palpitations, or frequent tiredness
- Exercise-induced fainting, giddiness, or collapse
- Insufficient weight gain (in children) or poor feeding (in infants)
- Blue discolouration of lips, fingers, or toes
- Family history of heart disease or sudden death under age 50
- Need for sports cardiology screening and advice
- Need for newborn or fetal screening for congenital heart disease
- Breathlessness out of proportion to activity level

### 4.14 Referral Guidelines (For Clinicians)

**Eyebrow:** `For Referring Clinicians`  
**Headline:** `When to refer to paediatric cardiology`  
**Lead:** Structured referral criteria to help GPs, paediatricians, and sports medicine physicians identify patients who would benefit from specialist cardiac evaluation.

**Present as an accordion/expandable list:**

1. **Chest Pain** — Exertional chest pain; associated syncope/palpitations/dyspnoea; abnormal ECG
2. **Syncope / Collapse** — During exercise; with palpitations; family history of cardiomyopathy/channelopathy
3. **Heart Murmurs** — Any murmur warrants evaluation; risk of missed HCM, ARVC, or coronary anomalies
4. **Palpitations** — During sport; associated dizziness; abnormal ECG findings
5. **Abnormal ECG/Echo** — T-wave inversion, ST depression, pathologic Q waves, pre-excitation, prolonged QT
6. **Sports Pre-Participation** — Exertional symptoms; prior CHD; hypertension stage 2; competitive clearance
7. **Myocarditis / Pericarditis** — Chest pain with elevated troponin; post-viral exercise intolerance; return-to-play decisions
8. **Hypertension** — BP ≥ 95th percentile on 3 visits; secondary hypertension; LVH on imaging
9. **Family History** — Sudden cardiac death < 50; HCM; DCM; ARVC; LQTS; CPVT; Brugada
10. **Exercise Intolerance** — Poor performance; excessive fatigue; dizziness; cyanosis
11. **Post-Cardiac Surgery / CHD Follow-up** — Ongoing monitoring; sports clearance post-repair
12. **General Congenital Cardiology** — Known CHD; blue discolouration; fetal screening; interventional procedures

**Red Flags callout box:**
> **🚨 Urgent Cardiology Review Required:** Exertional syncope · Exercise-induced chest pain · Palpitations with near syncope · Resting HR abnormalities not explained by training · Elevated troponin with symptoms · ECG abnormalities concerning for cardiomyopathy

## 5. Design Direction & UX Notes

### Visual Identity

| Element | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Primary Palette** | Warm parchment background (`#f4efe6`), deep ink (`#1b1a17`), burnt sienna accent (`#b04a2a`) |
| **Secondary Colours** | Teal (`#1f4a4a`), Gold (`#b58a3c`), Green (`#3f6b3a`), Deep accent (`#7a3119`) |
| **Typography** | Instrument Serif (headlines), Geist (body text), JetBrains Mono (labels/captions) |
| **Tone** | Editorial, warm, premium — like a high-end medical journal |
| **Style** | Clean, generous whitespace, no clinical sterility — feels warm and human |

### Micro-interactions & Animations

- **ECG heartbeat line** — animated SVG below hero with pulsing dot
- **Stats count-up** — numbers animate when scrolled into view
- **Clinic cards** — expand/collapse with smooth transition
- **Trust band** — continuous horizontal marquee scroll
- **Testimonial carousel** — auto-rotate every 8 seconds with manual dot navigation
- **Reveal animations** — sections fade-up on scroll (IntersectionObserver)
- **Nav bar** — becomes opaque/compact on scroll

### Responsive Breakpoints

- **Desktop:** 1280px max-width container, two-column hero layout
- **Tablet:** Single column hero, stacked clinic cards
- **Mobile:** Full-width sections, hamburger navigation, touch-friendly CTAs

### Accessibility

- Semantic HTML5 elements throughout
- ARIA labels on interactive elements
- Keyboard navigation support (Escape to close modals)
- Sufficient colour contrast ratios
- Alt text on all images
